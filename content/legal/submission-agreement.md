## 1. Introduction and contract formation

**1.1** This Submission Agreement is entered into between you ("you", "the Submitter", "the Customer") and MintVault Ltd ("MintVault", "we", "us", "our") each time you submit one or more Cards to MintVault for grading, authentication, reholder, crossover, review, or related services.

**1.2** A Submission Agreement is formed when all of the following occur:

(a) you complete a submission through the MintVault Site, select a Service Tier, declare values, accept these terms, and pay the applicable fees; and

(b) MintVault accepts the submission, which may occur by: (i) confirmation email; (ii) MintVault commencing the grading or other service; or (iii) another clear act of acceptance by MintVault.

Receipt of a Card alone does not constitute acceptance of a submission.

**1.3** Each submission forms a separate Submission Agreement. Where you submit multiple Cards under a single order, the Submission Agreement applies to each Card individually unless the context requires otherwise.

**1.4** This Submission Agreement sits alongside the Website Terms, the Guarantee & Correction Policy, the Privacy Policy, and the Shipping Requirements. Where there is a conflict in relation to a grading service or a Card in our custody, this Submission Agreement and the Guarantee & Correction Policy prevail over the Website Terms.

## 2. Definitions

**"Authentication"** — MintVault's professional assessment that a Card appears genuine based on visual, technical, and reference-data comparison at the time of assessment.

**"Card"** — the physical trading card (or cards) you submit to MintVault under the Submission Agreement.

**"Certificate"** — the digital record issued by MintVault for a graded Card, identified by a unique Certificate ID.

**"Crossover"** — the service where a Card currently in another grading company's slab is submitted to MintVault for grading and encapsulation in a MintVault slab.

**"Declared Value"** — the monetary value you assign to each Card at submission, reflecting your honest estimate of its current fair market value.

**"Document Reference Number"** — the confidential alphanumeric identifier associated with a Certificate, disclosed only on the Registered Keeper's Owner Copy and required, together with the Certificate ID, to initiate a Transfer.

**"Grading"** — MintVault's professional opinion of the condition of a Card at the time of assessment, expressed as an overall grade and, where relevant, sub-grades.

**"Guarantee & Correction Policy"** — MintVault's published guarantee policy covering remedies and claims.

**"Legal Owner"** — the person or entity holding lawful title to a Card under general law. The Legal Owner may or may not be the same person as the Registered Keeper.

**"Logbook"** — the live digital record of a Certificate, including grade data, images, Registered Keeper history, and related metadata, made available through the Site. The Owner Copy is a private variant containing the Document Reference Number, available only to the current Registered Keeper.

**"Registered Keeper"** — the person recorded by MintVault as currently responsible for a Certificate. The Registered Keeper is not, by virtue of that status alone, the Legal Owner of the Card (see clause 11).

**"Reholder"** — the service where a Card in a MintVault or other holder is removed and re-encapsulated in a new MintVault slab.

**"Service Tier"** — the service level you selected at submission (including pricing, turnaround, and maximum Declared Value).

**"Submission Materials"** — the images, scans, data, grading notes, slab photographs, QR and NFC records, Certificate and Logbook pages, and related submission data associated with a Card.

**"Transfer"** — the process by which the Registered Keeper of a Certificate is changed from one person to another through the official MintVault process.

**"Value Protection"** — MintVault's optional in-custody cover for loss or damage, purchased per Card at the tiers displayed on the Site at submission.

## 3. Scope of services

**3.1** MintVault offers the following services, as selected by you at submission:

(a) **Grading** — professional opinion of condition, authentication, encapsulation in a MintVault slab, issuance of a Certificate and Logbook.

(b) **Authentication** — assessment of whether a Card appears genuine, without a numerical grade.

(c) **Reholder** — removal of a Card from an existing holder and re-encapsulation in a MintVault slab.

(d) **Crossover** — submission of a Card in another grading company's holder for MintVault Grading and encapsulation.

(e) **Review** — internal re-examination of a previously graded Card.

(f) Such other services as MintVault may offer from time to time on the Site.

**3.2** Grading and Authentication are professional opinions, not guarantees. Grades and authentication determinations are subjective expert assessments at the time of assessment. They may differ from determinations made by other grading companies, and may change over time as industry standards evolve, new information emerges, or on re-examination.

**3.3** Service tier pricing, turnaround targets, and maximum Declared Values are as displayed on the Site at the time of submission. Turnaround targets are estimates, not guarantees (see clause 7).

## 4A. Compliance, identity verification, and AML

MintVault is committed to compliance with applicable UK law, including the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 ("POCA 2002"), the Money Laundering, Terrorist Financing and Transfer of Funds (Information on the Payer) Regulations 2017 ("MLR 2017"), the Fraud Act 2006, and sanctions legislation. MintVault may undertake identity, source-of-funds, and provenance verification in relation to submissions, particularly high-value submissions.

**4A.2** Identity verification — when required. MintVault may require you to complete identity verification ("Verification") before accepting, processing, returning, or issuing a Certificate for a submission where:

(a) the Declared Value of any single Card is £3,000 or more;

(b) the total Declared Value of the submission is £5,000 or more;

(c) MintVault reasonably believes Verification is appropriate based on the circumstances of the submission;

(d) MintVault is required to do so under MLR 2017; or

**4A.3** What Verification involves. Verification may include:

(a) providing government-issued photographic identification;

(b) providing proof of current address dated within the last three months;

(c) providing information about how you acquired the Card, including receipts or purchase correspondence;

(e) for business customers, providing company documentation, beneficial-ownership information, and evidence of authority to act.

**4A.4** Pause in services during Verification. MintVault may pause the services pending completion of satisfactory Verification. Pausing services in accordance with this clause 4A:

(a) does not constitute a breach of the Submission Agreement by MintVault;

(b) does not trigger any turnaround-time remedy under clause 7 or the Guarantee & Correction Policy; and

(c) does not affect your ability to cancel under clauses 6.4–6.5 (statutory cancellation rights).

MintVault will use reasonable endeavours to progress Verification promptly and will keep you informed of material delays.

**4A.5** Refusal or escalation. Where Verification is not satisfactorily completed within a reasonable period, or where MintVault has reasonable grounds to suspect money laundering, terrorist financing, fraud, theft, sanctions-listed parties, or other unlawful activity, MintVault may:

(a) refuse to proceed with the submission, return the Card (at the Customer's cost where the refusal relates to suspected unlawful activity), and refund grading fees proportionately in accordance with the Guarantee & Correction Policy;

Where MintVault makes or contemplates making a disclosure under POCA 2002 or MLR 2017, MintVault may be prohibited by law from disclosing the fact of the disclosure or the reasons for any delay to the Customer. **4A.7** Data protection. Personal data collected under this clause 4A is processed in accordance with the Privacy Policy and MLR 2017, and is retained for the periods required by that legislation (generally five years from the end of the business relationship).

**4A.8** Costs of Verification. MintVault bears the routine costs of Verification. Where Verification requires exceptional effort (for example, international document legalisation or multi-party enquiries), MintVault may pass on reasonable costs to the Customer with prior notice.

## 5. Declared Value and auto-upcharge

**5.1** You must assign a Declared Value to each Card at submission, reflecting your honest and reasonable estimate of the Card's current fair market value. You may obtain professional valuations if you are unsure.

**5.2** What the Declared Value determines.

(a) The Service Tier you are eligible to select (each tier has a maximum Declared Value cap).

(b) The Value Protection tier and any applicable surcharge.

(c) The cap on MintVault's liability for loss or damage in custody (see clause 9.4 and the Guarantee & Correction Policy).

(d) Market value fluctuation. Declared Value reflects your estimate of fair market value at submission. MintVault's cover and liability are based on Declared Value and the Value Protection tier purchased, and are not adjusted for subsequent market movements during custody. If you anticipate significant market movement, you may declare a value reflecting your assessment of value at the likely point of loss, provided the overall valuation remains honest and reasonable.

**5.3** Auto-upcharge. If MintVault reasonably determines, at receipt or during grading, that a Card's fair market value materially exceeds the Declared Value, MintVault may:

(a) re-tier the submission to an appropriate Service Tier and recalculate the fee;

(b) recalculate applicable Value Protection surcharges; and

(c) notify you of the revised amount and request payment before grading proceeds or the Card is returned.

**5.4** Where you do not pay a reasonable re-tier charge within a reasonable time, MintVault may:

(a) pause the submission and retain the Card until payment is received, subject to clause 18;

(b) return the Card ungraded at your cost; or

(c) grade the Card at the originally selected tier, in which case MintVault's liability for loss or damage remains capped at the originally selected tier and Declared Value.

**5.5** Effect of under-declaration.

(a) If MintVault determines that Declared Value was deliberately or recklessly understated, Value Protection cover above the Declared Value is void and the cap is the Declared Value.

(b) If MintVault determines that Declared Value was carelessly or innocently understated, MintVault may reduce the cover proportionately to reflect the correct value, but will not withhold cover entirely.

(c) Where MintVault identifies material under-declaration before grading, the primary remedy is auto-upcharge under clause 5.3 rather than reduction of cover.

**6.4** Consumer right to cancel

Where you are a consumer, you have a statutory right to cancel the Submission Agreement without giving a reason within 14 days ("Cancellation Period") starting on the day after the Submission Agreement is formed under clause 1.2, under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional Charges) Regulations 2013.

**6.5** Services commencing within the Cancellation Period

(a) If you ask MintVault to commence the service during the Cancellation Period, you give express consent to commencement and acknowledge that you may lose the right to cancel once the service has been fully performed.

(b) Calculation of the amount payable on cancellation during service performance. If you exercise the right to cancel before MintVault has fully performed the service, the amount payable will be calculated in accordance with regulation 36(4) of the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, being an amount in proportion to what has been performed by MintVault in comparison with the full coverage of the contract. In calculating this proportionate amount, MintVault will take into account: (i) the stage of the grading reached at cancellation; (ii) reasonable costs already incurred attributable to the submission; (iii) any auto-upcharge or additional fees properly incurred; and (iv) the total contract price. If the total price is excessive, the proportionate amount will be calculated on the market value of the service performed, in accordance with regulation 36(5).

(c) MintVault will refund amounts paid, less any deductions permitted under clause 6.5(b), within 14 days of receiving your cancellation notice, or (if later) within 14 days of receiving the returned Card.

(d) Once the service has been fully performed, the statutory right to cancel no longer applies.

(e) Your other statutory rights — including the right to services performed with reasonable care and skill under section 49 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 — are not affected.

## 8. Refusal, no-grade, altered, counterfeit, and stolen Cards

**8.1** Right of refusal. MintVault is not obligated to grade every Card submitted. MintVault may refuse to grade, decline to encapsulate, return the Card raw, or retain the Card for investigation where, in its reasonable professional opinion: (a) the Card appears counterfeit, doctored, restored, trimmed, recoloured, or otherwise altered; (b) the Card has been tampered with or is unsafe to process; (c) the Card is reported stolen or MintVault reasonably suspects it may be stolen; (d) the Card is the subject of an ownership dispute, law-enforcement enquiry, or court order; (e) the Card falls outside MintVault's accepted categories; (f) the submission breaches clause 4.1, clause 4A, or any other provision; (g) grading would expose MintVault to legal or regulatory risk; or (h) the submission was made by a person whose account is suspended.

**8.2** No-grade outcome. Where MintVault refuses to grade, MintVault may return the Card ungraded at MintVault's cost (refusal solely on category or operational grounds) or at the Customer's cost (refusal relating to alteration, counterfeit, or warranty breach), subject to clause 8.4.

**8.3** Retention for investigation. Where MintVault reasonably suspects a Card has been altered, tampered with, stolen, or is subject to a law-enforcement enquiry, MintVault may retain the Card for a reasonable period for investigation. Retention: (a) will last no longer than reasonably necessary; (b) will not exceed 30 days without MintVault providing the Customer with a written explanation; and (c) will not exceed 90 days without an active requirement imposed by a court order, law-enforcement request, regulator, or specific legal obligation.

**8.4** Reporting. MintVault may report suspected counterfeit, stolen, or unlawfully held Cards to relevant authorities, industry bodies, insurers, or affected parties, where reasonably necessary for legal compliance, fraud investigation, protection of users or third parties, or evidence preservation.

**8.5** Fees on refusal. Where MintVault refuses to grade, the grading fee is refundable only to the extent set out in the Guarantee & Correction Policy.

## 9. Shipping, custody, and Value Protection

**9.1** Shipping to MintVault. You are responsible for arranging shipping to MintVault. Risk of loss or damage in transit to MintVault remains with you until MintVault confirms receipt. You must follow the minimum inbound shipping standards set out in the Shipping Requirements; failure to do so may reduce cover.

**9.2** While in MintVault's custody. From confirmed receipt until confirmed dispatch, the Card is in MintVault's custody and MintVault will take reasonable care of it. Value Protection, if purchased, applies during this period.

**9.3** Return shipping. MintVault arranges return shipping in accordance with the Shipping Requirements. The required tier is determined by the value of Cards being returned. Cover during return shipping is as set out in the Shipping Requirements; above the cover level, the Customer's risk unless additional cover has been separately purchased.

**9.4** Value Protection cover and custody cap

(a) **Purpose.** This clause sets out the maximum amount MintVault will pay you for loss, damage, or destruction of a Card in MintVault's custody, subject to clause 9.4(f) and clause 22.1.

(b) **How the cap is calculated.** The custody cap for each Card is the Value Protection tier you purchased for that Card at submission, up to a maximum of £7,500 per Card (the "Maximum Standard Cover"). Tiers currently available: Standard cover up to £500; Enhanced cover up to £1,500; Premium cover up to £3,000; Maximum Standard cover up to £7,500. Surcharges per tier as displayed on the Site.

(c) **Declared Value floor.** If Declared Value is higher than the Value Protection tier purchased, the cap remains the Value Protection tier — you cannot claim above the cover you paid for. If Declared Value is lower than the Value Protection tier, the cap is the Value Protection tier purchased, provided you have not materially under-declared in breach of clauses 4.1(e) and 5.5.

(d) **Effect of under-declaration.** As set out in clause 5.5.

(e) **Bespoke arrangements for high-value Cards.** Where Declared Value exceeds £7,500, you must contact MintVault before submission to agree bespoke handling arrangements in writing. In the absence of a written bespoke agreement, the Maximum Standard Cover applies.

(f) **What happens if MintVault causes loss through lack of reasonable care.**

*In plain English:* If we negligently lose or damage your Card, the cap in this clause 9.4 does not apply, and we remain responsible to you under section 49 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015. The cap applies to loss from accidents and events where we have taken reasonable care. It does not apply where we have failed to take reasonable care.

*In legal terms:* Nothing in this clause 9.4 excludes or limits MintVault's liability to provide remedies for breach of section 49 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, including remedies under sections 54 to 56 of that Act, or any liability which cannot be limited under UK law. In such cases, MintVault's liability will be as required by the Consumer Rights Act 2015, without the cap in this clause 9.4 applying.

**9.5** Clause 9.4 does not exclude liability for death or personal injury caused by MintVault's negligence, fraud, or any other liability that cannot be limited under UK law.

**9.6** Claims for loss or damage are handled under the Guarantee & Correction Policy.

## 11. Registered Keeper and Legal Owner

**11.1** The Registered Keeper is not necessarily the Legal Owner. The Registered Keeper is the person recorded by MintVault as currently responsible for a Certificate. Legal ownership is determined by general law and may depend on factors outside MintVault's records.

**11.2** The Logbook is an evidential platform record. It is evidence of the matters recorded at the time of recording. It is not, by itself, unconditional proof of legal title to the Card.

**11.3** Live Logbook is the primary current status record. Cached pages and previously downloaded PDFs may be out of date.

**11.4** Historic entries and current status are separate. Historic entries remain in the record. Personal data within historic records is retained per the Privacy Policy retention schedule (pseudonymous identifiers retained indefinitely; identifiable personal data retained for the periods set out there).

**11.5** MintVault is a record-keeper. MintVault is not an arbiter of legal title. Disputes must be resolved between the parties, by the courts, or through agreed ADR. MintVault will comply with valid court orders.

## 12. The Document Reference Number

**12.2** The Document Reference Number is required, with the Certificate ID, to initiate a Transfer.

**12.3** You must keep the Document Reference Number confidential.

**12.7** Compromise-report response time. MintVault will apply a freeze within a reasonable period of receiving a credible compromise report, typically within one business hour during working hours and within four hours outside working hours.

## 13. Transfers

**13.1** Transfers must use the official MintVault process.

**13.2** Transfer flow. **13.6** Buyer due diligence. Verify cryptographic signature; confirm Logbook Version; never pay before receiving the Transfer email; report suspected fraud.

**13.7** Refusal or freeze under clause 15, subject to the Silent Freeze Fairness Framework in clause 15A.

**14.1** A Transfer may be disputed within 14 days of the later of the two confirmations.

**14.2** Grounds include: fraudulent initiation; stolen Card; payment not received or reversed; material misrepresentation; court order requiring reversal.

**14.3** Disputes raised by emailing support@mintvaultuk.com with Certificate ID, grounds, and evidence. Personal data processed in disputes is retained per the Privacy Policy retention schedule.

**14.4** MintVault's determinations relate to the platform record and do not resolve underlying legal disputes.

**14.5** Post-window disputes may still be investigated but the Transfer is treated as final unless MintVault determines otherwise.

## 15. Freezing, correction, amendment, and deactivation

**15.1** MintVault may freeze, flag, amend, correct, or deactivate a Certificate or Registered Keeper entry where: (a) Card reported stolen; (b) MintVault suspects fraud, tampering, counterfeit, or integrity issue; (c) ownership dispute or claim raised; (d) payment reversed; (e) data error identified; (f) court order or law-enforcement request applies; (g) safeguarding reasons; or (h) the Card has been cracked or removed from its slab.

**15.2** Correction of mistakes. Where corrections materially affect Registered Keeper or grade, MintVault will notify affected parties.

**15.3** Historic record integrity. Corrections do not remove historic entries; superseded entries may be annotated.

**15.4** Notification, review, and appeal are governed by clause 15A.

**15.5** Deactivation does not, by itself, change the physical condition, grade, or authenticity of the Card.

## 15A. Silent Freeze Fairness Framework

**15A.1** Default notification period. MintVault will notify the Registered Keeper of a freeze within 7 days, unless an exception in 15A.2 applies.

**15A.2** Extended silent period — exceptions. Notification may be delayed where: (a) it would defeat an active law-enforcement investigation, or MintVault has made/contemplates a POCA 2002 disclosure (tipping-off restrictions); (b) it would prejudice a court order or regulatory enquiry; or (c) it would put a third party at material risk of harm or continued loss.

**15A.3** Maximum silent period. Where 15A.2 applies, MintVault reviews the need for continued silence every 30 days. MintVault will not maintain silence for more than 90 days without an active law-enforcement, court, or regulatory requirement.

**15A.4** On notification. MintVault will provide: (a) the fact of the freeze; (b) the general reason to the extent lawful; (c) contact route for the Registered Keeper; (d) the review and appeal process; and (e) where known, expected duration.

**15A.5** Review and appeal. Email support@mintvaultuk.com with subject "FREEZE REVIEW — [Certificate ID]". MintVault will: (a) acknowledge within 3 working days; (b) conduct internal review by a senior team member, independent where reasonably practicable; (c) communicate outcome within 14 working days; (d) provide reasons; (e) lift or modify the freeze promptly where appropriate.

**15A.6** Effect on statutory rights. This clause does not limit statutory rights, including ICO complaint and legal proceedings.

**15A.7** Service credits for prolonged freeze resolved in keeper's favour. Where a freeze of more than 30 days is resolved in the Registered Keeper's favour, MintVault will offer a service credit or refund of relevant fees, without admission of liability.

## 16. Reholder and Crossover
