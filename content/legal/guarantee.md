## 1. What this Policy covers

**1.1** This Guarantee & Correction Policy ("Policy") explains what MintVault does and does not guarantee in relation to its grading, authentication, and related services, and the process for raising claims and obtaining remedies.

**1.2** This Policy forms part of the contract between you and Mint Vault UK Ltd ("MintVault") and applies alongside the Website Terms, the Submission Agreement, the Privacy Policy, and the Shipping Requirements. Defined terms used here have the meanings given in the Submission Agreement.

**1.3** Your statutory rights under UK consumer law — including the Consumer Rights Act 2015, the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, and related legislation — are not affected by this Policy. Where this Policy offers remedies that exceed statutory rights, those remedies are given in addition to and do not replace your statutory rights.

## 2. Grading is a professional opinion

**2.1** *In plain English:* MintVault's grades and authentication determinations are the considered professional opinion of our graders at the time of assessment, using industry-standard methodology and reference data. They are opinions, not guarantees of condition or authenticity for all time and all purposes. A different grader, a different grading company, or a re-examination at a later date may reach a different view.

**2.2** MintVault does not guarantee:

(a) that a grade we assign will match the grade assigned by any other grading company;

(b) that a Certificate or grade will maintain or increase in market value;

(c) that re-examination, regrade, or industry-standard changes will produce the same result;

(d) subjective aspects of condition beyond the graded data points (for example, eye appeal, registry-set preferences, or collector-specific criteria);

(e) the market acceptance, liquidity, or resale price of any Card; or

(f) the accuracy, availability, or performance of third-party systems used alongside the Logbook.

**2.3** MintVault performs services with reasonable care and skill, as required by section 49 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015. A difference of grading opinion is not, by itself, a failure to perform with reasonable care and skill. A claim that MintVault has failed to perform with reasonable care and skill is handled under clause 10 of this Policy.

## 3. Authentication

**3.1** MintVault's authentication is a professional opinion, based on visual, technical, and reference-data comparison, that a Card appears to be genuine at the time of assessment. It is not an unconditional warranty of authenticity.

**3.2** Where MintVault later becomes aware, or is provided with credible evidence, that a Card authenticated by MintVault is in fact counterfeit, reproduction, or materially altered, the remedies in clause 9 apply.

## 4. Encapsulation and slab integrity

**4.1** MintVault slabs are designed to be tamper-evident and to protect the Card under normal handling and storage conditions. Slabs may crack, scuff, fog, or show minor surface marks as a result of ordinary handling, shipping, or storage; these do not, by themselves, indicate tampering and do not affect the underlying Certificate.

**4.2** If a slab shows signs of opening, cracking inconsistent with normal handling, or clear tampering, you should contact support@mintvaultuk.com without delay. MintVault may offer a reholder (see clause 12) at its discretion and on such terms as are reasonable in the circumstances.

**4.3** Where slab damage occurs in MintVault's custody, it is handled under clause 9.4 of the Submission Agreement (Value Protection and custody cap).

## 5. Label, data, and Certificate corrections

**5.1** Clerical and data errors. MintVault may correct clerical or data errors on Certificates and Logbook entries (for example, misspellings, incorrect set identifiers, or mis-matched card-identity data). Where a correction materially affects the grade, the Card identity, or the Registered Keeper record, MintVault will notify affected parties.

**5.2** How to report. Report suspected label or data errors to support@mintvaultuk.com with: (a) the Certificate ID; (b) a photograph of the slab and label clearly showing the issue; (c) a description of the error; (d) supporting reference data where available.

**5.3** Remedy. Where MintVault confirms an error, MintVault will, at its reasonable discretion: (a) correct the Certificate, Logbook, and related records; (b) issue an updated Certificate and slab (with reholder) at no additional charge where the error was MintVault's; (c) arrange for return of the Card for physical correction where necessary; (d) update QR / NFC records as needed.

**5.4** Historic integrity. Corrections do not remove historic Logbook entries. MintVault may annotate superseded entries so the current position is clear.

## 6. Mechanical and grading errors

**6.1** Grading review. If you believe a Card has been graded on a basis that is clearly inconsistent with MintVault's published grading standard (a "mechanical error"), you may request a review by emailing support@mintvaultuk.com within 30 days of delivery with: (a) the Certificate ID; (b) clear photographs of the slab and Card; (c) a description of the suspected error; (d) reference to the specific grading criterion affected.

**6.2** Review process. MintVault will: (a) acknowledge within 3 working days; (b) conduct internal review by a senior grader, independent of the original grader where reasonably practicable; (c) communicate outcome within 14 working days; (d) provide a written explanation.

**6.3** Outcomes. If MintVault confirms a mechanical error, MintVault will at its reasonable discretion: regrade the Card, correct the Certificate, arrange reholder at no additional charge, or offer another appropriate remedy. A difference of grading opinion, as distinct from a mechanical error, is not a ground for obligatory correction (see clause 2).

## 7. Shipping and return

**7.1** Shipping to MintVault, custody during grading, and return shipping are governed by clause 9 of the Submission Agreement and the Shipping Requirements. Summary: (a) inbound shipping risk is with the Customer; (b) custody while in MintVault's care is covered by Value Protection to the tier purchased, subject to clause 9.4 of the Submission Agreement; (c) outbound return shipping cover is determined by the value of Cards being returned and the shipping tier applicable under the Shipping Requirements; (d) failure to follow the minimum inbound shipping standards may reduce cover.

**7.2** Claims for loss or damage in return shipping are handled under clause 14 of this Policy, within the 14-day inspection window.

## 8. Turnaround and delay remedies

**8.1** Turnaround times displayed on the Site are targets. MintVault uses reasonable endeavours to meet displayed turnaround times but does not guarantee them.

**8.2** Goodwill credit for material delay. Where turnaround materially exceeds the displayed target and no exception in 8.3 applies, MintVault will, as a gesture of goodwill: (a) refund any priority / expedited uplift paid; (b) where the delay is substantial, offer a service credit or partial refund of the grading fee on a proportionate basis. Goodwill credits are without admission of liability and do not replace your statutory rights.

**8.3** Exceptions — no delay remedy. A delay will not trigger the goodwill remedy in 8.2 where caused by: (a) Verification under clause 4A SA (AML / identity); (b) retention for investigation under clause 8.3 SA; (c) a freeze, dispute, or deactivation under clauses 15 or 15A SA; (d) auto-upcharge handling where the Customer has not yet paid the revised amount; (e) Customer conduct; (f) force majeure or events outside MintVault's reasonable control; (g) a bespoke arrangement with different turnaround expectations.

**8.4** Statutory rights preserved. Nothing in clause 8 affects your right under section 52 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015 to performance within a reasonable time.

## 9. Refusal, no-grade, and counterfeit findings

**9.1** Refusal and no-grade generally. MintVault's right to refuse grading, assign a no-grade outcome, or retain a Card for investigation is set out in clause 8 of the Submission Agreement.

**9.2** Refund on refusal or no-grade. Where MintVault refuses to grade, assigns a no-grade outcome, or (in the case of a confirmed counterfeit, altered, or stolen Card) does not proceed, MintVault will refund a proportionate amount of the grading fee reflecting the work not performed and the reasonable costs MintVault has incurred up to that point. The refund will be calculated in accordance with the proportionate-amount principle in regulation 36(4) of the Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013 where applicable, and will generally be substantial but may be reduced where: (a) MintVault has incurred investigation costs directly attributable to the submission; (b) return shipping, handling, or administrative costs properly apply; (c) the Customer is in breach of clause 4.1 or clause 4A of the Submission Agreement; (d) the Card is retained lawfully under clause 8.3 of the Submission Agreement.

**9.3** No refund where MintVault retains the Card for investigation. Where a Card is retained under clause 8.3 SA or under a legal obligation, refunds are considered once the retention is resolved.

**9.4** Auto-upcharge refusals. Where a submission is returned because the Customer has not paid a reasonable auto-upcharge, MintVault may apply the refund calculation above and may recover reasonable costs.

> **SOLICITOR REVIEW FLAG — Clause 9:** Please confirm proportionate-refund wording (replacing the prior fixed-50% approach) aligns with s.49 CRA 2015 and reg 36(4) Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013.

## 10. Failure to perform with reasonable care and skill

**10.1** If MintVault has failed to perform services with reasonable care and skill (section 49, Consumer Rights Act 2015), you may be entitled to remedies under sections 54 to 56 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015, including the right to require repeat performance or an appropriate price reduction.

**10.2** Reasonable care and skill claims override the custody cap. Where a claim arises from MintVault's failure to perform with reasonable care and skill, the Value Protection cap in clause 9.4 of the Submission Agreement does not apply (see clause 9.4(f) of the Submission Agreement). Remedies are as required by the Consumer Rights Act 2015.

**10.3** How to raise a claim. Claims under this clause 10 are raised under the general claim process in clause 14 of this Policy, with clear reference to the alleged failure to perform with reasonable care and skill.

## 11. Loss, damage, or theft in MintVault's custody

**11.1** Where a Card is lost, damaged, or destroyed in MintVault's custody, the primary remedy is under clause 9.4 of the Submission Agreement (Value Protection and custody cap), except where clause 10 applies (failure of reasonable care and skill).

**11.2** How MintVault calculates the payout. Where the custody cap applies, the amount MintVault will pay is the lesser of: (a) the Card's Declared Value (subject to clause 5.5 SA on under-declaration); and (b) the Value Protection tier purchased. Subject to the Maximum Standard Cover of £7,500 per Card unless a higher amount has been separately agreed in writing (clause 9.4(e) SA).

**11.3** Supporting evidence. For custody claims you should provide: (a) photographs of the slab and Card before shipping; (b) receipts or valuations supporting the Declared Value; (c) correspondence evidencing the loss or damage; (d) a crime reference number where theft is involved.

**11.4** Payment. Where a custody claim is accepted, MintVault will pay within 30 days of acceptance, by the same method as the original payment unless otherwise agreed.

**11.5** Assignment of rights. On payment of a custody claim, MintVault may require the Customer to assign to MintVault any rights the Customer has against third parties (for example, carriers) in relation to the loss or damage, so that MintVault may pursue those rights in subrogation.

## 12. Reholder and reissue

**12.1** Reholder. Where a slab is damaged in MintVault's custody, contains a correctable error, or otherwise qualifies under this Policy, MintVault may offer a reholder at no additional charge or at a reduced charge as appropriate.

**12.2** Reissue of Certificate or Document Reference Number. Where the Document Reference Number is compromised or needs to be reissued for legitimate reasons (clause 12.6 SA), MintVault will reissue on identity verification. A reasonable administrative fee may apply where the reissue is for the Customer's convenience rather than a security or MintVault-caused reason.

## 13. Freezes, deactivations, and appeals

Freezes, deactivations, and the associated fairness framework are governed by clause 15A of the Submission Agreement (Silent Freeze Fairness Framework), including 7-day default notification, 30-day review cycles, 90-day hard ceiling subject to exceptions, and the appeal process. This Policy does not replace those provisions; it operates alongside them.

Where a freeze is ultimately resolved in the Registered Keeper's favour and exceeded 30 days, MintVault will consider a service credit as set out in clause 15A.7 of the Submission Agreement.

## 14. How to make a claim

**14.1** Where to send claims. All claims under this Policy are raised by emailing support@mintvaultuk.com with a clear subject line identifying the claim type (for example, "CLAIM — [Certificate ID] — Shipping damage").

**14.2** What to include. Provide: (a) the Certificate ID (where applicable); (b) a description of the issue; (c) supporting photographs, documentation, and evidence; (d) the remedy you are seeking; (e) relevant dates.

**14.3** Claim windows.

(a) Shipping / condition on return: within 14 days of delivery (clause 10, Submission Agreement).

(b) Label, data, or Certificate errors: as soon as reasonably practicable after the error is identified.

(c) Mechanical grading errors: within 30 days of delivery (clause 6.1).

(d) Reasonable care and skill claims (clause 10): any time within the statutory limitation period.

(e) Loss, damage, or theft in custody: on discovery; where discovered only on return, within the 14-day return-inspection window.

**14.4** Acknowledgement and resolution timeline. MintVault will: (a) acknowledge within 3 working days; (b) indicate whether further information is required; (c) aim to resolve within 30 calendar days of receipt of a complete claim and supporting evidence, subject to investigation requirements; (d) provide a written outcome with reasons.

**14.5** If you disagree with the outcome. You may: (a) request internal escalation to a senior team member; (b) raise the matter through the ADR route in clause 15; (c) pursue your statutory rights through the courts.

## 15. Dispute resolution

**15.1** MintVault aims to resolve complaints through clause 14. Where resolution is not possible, the following applies.

**15.2** Courts. You may bring proceedings in the courts of England and Wales, except that consumers resident in Scotland or Northern Ireland may bring proceedings in their local courts.

**15.3** Free consumer advice. UK consumers can seek free advice from Citizens Advice (citizensadvice.org.uk, 0800 144 8848) or local Trading Standards. The ICO (ico.org.uk) handles data-protection complaints.

**15.4** Statutory rights. Nothing in this Policy excludes or restricts your statutory rights.
